my work

UI Branding for Magnolia Cafe

Branding Research: A compiled brief for the client "Magnolia Cafe" who are focus on sustainable and ethical practices. Conducting marktet research on competitors and similar existing platforms of mobile ordering via app. Conducted a SWOT analysis for each competiting company. In conclusion, the research should provide insight on what kind of features to include on the app.

Personas: The target audience is for anyone from 18-40 years old. Ideally the customer is someone who has a steady income and can afford to buy a coffee everyday. We hope that this person understands our clients vision and approach of sustainability and mindfulness.

Moodboards: All of the moodboards contain some variation of yellow, orange or green. Using colour theory in relation to emotions; these colours represent hunger, happiness, optimism, nature, eco, health and motivation. The theme and colour palette of the first board is earth and neutral tones, the coziness of a cafe. The second theme is a urban/contemporary style with pops of colour. Lastly, a minimalistic style with metallic accents for a luxurious feel.

Low Fidelity Wireframes: I created some base wireframes for Magnolia's mobile ordering app. The app features a landing page, home page with option to sign up/login, rewards and payment page, menu, ordering page, checkout and profile.

High Fidelity Mock Up: The finished high fidelity mock up for Magnolia. The inspiration of the name ties back the Chinese heritage where magnolias are believed to symbolize longevity, beauty and perseverance. For the colour palette, we chose to combine the minimalistic style with warm pops of colour. We chose yellow to symobolize the warmth, hunger and happiness with food. It also represents the sun and how it is a cruical part of the plant life cycle.

UI Branding for Fictitious Companies

Lunar: This is a web page for a home decor brand called, Lunar. The company offers high-end home decor, I chose a minimalistic and slight editorial approach compared to many companies such as West Elm, Wayfair, Ikea etc.

Breeze: A mobile webpage for a fictitious sustainable skincare and wellness brand called, Breeze. The mockups I designed features a home page, product list and two product pages with descriptions. Self-care and wellness has become increasely important during the pandemic; remember to take a deep breathe, relax and take care of yourself mentally and physically. Breeze promotes self-love, positivity, ethical and sustainable pratices.

Flux: A magazine mock up for Flux. Flux has a minimalistic, modern style that releases bi-monthly issues that features travel tips and information of different cities around the world.
